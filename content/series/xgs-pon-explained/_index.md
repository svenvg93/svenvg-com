XGS-PON is the 10-gigabit symmetric PON standard behind most new fiber-to-the-home deployments. This series explains how it works from the ground up — the physical architecture (OLT, passive splitter, ONT, and wavelength-division multiplexing on a single fiber strand), then the protocol layer (PLOAM registration, GEM port encapsulation, T-CONT QoS classes, and dynamic bandwidth allocation). Written for homelab users and network engineers who want to understand what is actually happening below the Ethernet interface on their fiber connection.
